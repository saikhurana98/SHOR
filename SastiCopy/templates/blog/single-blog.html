Why wear streetwear in India?

Streetwear in India is still a fairly niche market, and many Indians do not understand the appeal of the unique and jarring styles that streetwear employs. We asked one of our customers, Aditi, to shed some light on the spirit of streetwear and to explain what streetwear means to her.

Bablu Yadav: Why do you love wearing streetwear?

Aditi: One thing I appreciate about wearing streetwear in India is the fact that it’s so unique right now. Streetwear is all about expressing ourselves with no bars held. We wear streetwear only for ourselves; it’s not to appease our employers or to fit in with society. I think streetwear allows me to create my own personal style, to an extent that more conventional clothing doesn’t allow.

Bablu Yadav: Why do you think streetwear hasn’t taken off in India yet? Aditi: I mean, India is obviously still a very conservative place and people, especially women, feel a lot of pressure to act and dress a certain way. I actually think that’s something streetwear could help with. It pushes the boundaries of what society deems as acceptable, and I think a lot of Indians could benefit by questioning the constraints that are placed on us. Obviously it’s only clothing, but even these little things make a difference.
